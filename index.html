A History of Light
BY MARK KIRSCHEN
Through the telescope: Crater of the moon The rings around Saturn
A light floating in Radio waves of the first explosion As I stepped back, an image Quick constellation of a figure I knew Traced itself for a moment Among the night sky fires I remembered the search For a lost girl, years before In the forests of upstate New York: How we looked down thin paths Ended by walls of mist Lacing itself in layers between The trees How we were thanked and sent Away: only the locals would keep looking Now I had found her But could never bring her back: The angle of sight lost Light-years between her fingertips: I had found her But can never bring her back Mark Kirschen, "A History of Light" from Montemora Magazine.
